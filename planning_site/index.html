Relevant Implications

Relevant Implications are the possible effects or issues that may result from the creation of a digital product. There are three main relevant implications I have identified as pertinent for this database. These include future proofing, functionality and usability.

Future Proofing
Description Addressal
Future proofing refers to the ease at which a digital product can be updated. A digital product with a high degree of future proofing can be updated easily, would likely use technology that will be available in the future and will follow widely-understood conventions so that it is easily understood and changeable by others.

Functionality
Description Addressal
Functionality is the level of efficiency by which a digital product performs its function. High functionality describes a flexible digital product which works properly. In regards to a database, high functionality involves providing correct and consistently-formatted results for all queries and containing accurate data (ie. without errors).

Usability
Description Addressal
Usability refers to the degree of difficulty a user experiences during their interaction with a digital product. High usability describes a digital product that is ‘user-friendly’, providing clear instructions that are easy for the user to understand and outputs that do not use complex or technical language.
